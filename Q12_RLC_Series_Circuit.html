Q.12 — Series RLC Circuit Analysis

Unit 3 – AC Circuits (Single Phase)  |  5 Marks  |  CO3  |  Bloom's L3

R = 5 Ω L = 0.1 H C = 150 µF V = 200 V f = 50 Hz

📋 Given Data

Resistance  R = 5 Ω
Inductance  L = 0.1 H
Capacitance  C = 150 µF
Voltage  V = 200 V
Frequency  f = 50 Hz
Supply  1-Phase AC

🔌 Circuit Diagram

🧮 Step-by-Step Solution

i
Inductive Reactance (XL)
Unit: Ω
Formula: XL = 2πfL
Sub: XL = 2 × π × 50 × 0.1
XL = 2 × 3.1416 × 50 × 0.1 = 10π
✅ XL = 31.41 Ω
💡 Remember: XL increases with frequency (more frequency → more opposition from inductor)
ii
Capacitive Reactance (XC)
Unit: Ω
Formula: XC = 1 / (2πfC)
Sub: XC = 1 / (2 × π × 50 × 150 × 10⁻⁶)
XC = 1 / (0.04712) = 21.22 Ω
✅ XC = 21.22 Ω
💡 Remember: XC decreases with frequency (more frequency → less opposition from capacitor)
iii
Net Reactance (X)
Unit: Ω
Formula: X = XL − XC
Sub: X = 31.41 − 21.22
✅ X = 10.19 Ω (Inductive)
💡 Since XL > XC, the circuit is Inductive in nature → current lags behind voltage
iv
Impedance (Z)
Unit: Ω
Formula: Z = √(R² + X²)
Sub: Z = √(5² + 10.19²) = √(25 + 103.84) = √128.84
✅ Z = 11.35 Ω
💡 Impedance Triangle: Z is the hypotenuse, R is base, X is perpendicular. Think of Pythagoras theorem!
v
Current (I)
Unit: A
Formula: I = V / Z
Sub: I = 200 / 11.35
✅ I = 17.62 A
💡 Like Ohm's Law — higher impedance means lower current!
vi
Power Factor (cos φ)
Dimensionless
Formula: cos φ = R / Z
Sub: cos φ = 5 / 11.35
Angle: φ = cos⁻¹(0.4405) = 63.86°
✅ cos φ = 0.4405 (Lagging)
💡 Lagging because XL > XC — current lags voltage by 63.86°
vii
Active Power (P)
Unit: W (Watts)
Formula: P = V × I × cos φ   OR   P = I² × R
Method 1: P = 200 × 17.62 × 0.4405 = 1552 W
Method 2: P = (17.62)² × 5 = 310.46 × 5 = 1552 W ✓
✅ P = 1552 W ≈ 1.552 kW
💡 Active power = Real power = Useful work done. Only resistance dissipates power!
viii
Reactive Power (Q)
Unit: VAR
Formula: Q = V × I × sin φ   OR   Q = I² × X
sin φ: sin φ = X / Z = 10.19 / 11.35 = 0.8978
Method 1: Q = 200 × 17.62 × 0.8978 = 3163 VAR
Method 2: Q = (17.62)² × 10.19 = 310.46 × 10.19 = 3164 VAR ✓
✅ Q = 3163 VAR ≈ 3.163 kVAR
💡 Reactive power = Wattless power. It is stored & returned by L and C — no actual work done!

🧠 Quick Memory Tips (Remember These!)

⚡
ELI the ICE man

ELI → In an Linductor, Evoltage leads Icurrent.
ICE → In a Capacitor, Icurrent leads Evoltage.

📐
Impedance Triangle

Z² = R² + X² — Just like a right-angled triangle (Pythagoras). Z is the hypotenuse!

🔋
Power Triangle

S² = P² + Q²
S = Apparent Power (VA)
P = Active/Real Power (W)
Q = Reactive Power (VAR)

🏆
Nature of Circuit

XL > XC → Inductive (current lags)
XC > XL → Capacitive (current leads)
XL = XC → Resonance (Z = R min)
